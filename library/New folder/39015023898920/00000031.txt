The simple constructions given above provide quick constructions for some of the 100 parametric speci-
fications for triangular designs with r, k≤ 10 and listed in Tables X and XI. Other triangular designs, as
well as some of these simple ones, may also be constructed by mathematically sophisticated procedures.
Appropriate references to sources are given in Table X.
BCS (1954) provided 36 triangular designs having 3 ≤ k ≤ 10 and 2 < r < 10. Clatworthy (1956) was able
to expand the original listing to 76 designs by construction of new designs with k=2 and 3 and by including
n-plicates of solutions provided by BCS (1954). In the early 1960's three researchers at Peking University,
under the leadership of C. W. Liu, constructed many new triangular designs which have been included,
and Masuyama (1965) published new procedures for constructing triangular designs, several of which
were published the same year by the Chinese group. Shrikhande (1965), Ray-Chaudhuri (1962), (1965),
Seiden (1961), (1963) also constructed new triangular designs. Using a newly constructed D(1) design with
v=b=56,r=k=11 and λ=2 by Hall, Lane and Wales (1968), the author has constructed the new triangular
design T91.
It can be shown that 57 of the 100 parametric specifications for triangular designs given in Tables X
and XI lead to plans that are unique (up to an isomorphism). Of the remaining 43 parametric specifications
nonuniqueness has been established in 11 cases by the construction of 2 or 3 distinct plans for each.
Due to the fact that several of the triangular designs are of the linked blocks type, they are related to
balanced incomplete block designs through their duals, and consequently several others have duals which
are SGD designs. Among the triangular designs there exists ample evidence that a symmetrical triangular
design is not necessarily self-dual or truly self-dual as there are 5 cases for which the duals are SGD designs.
There are others which are truly self-dual and still others for which the weaker assertion, self-dual, can be
made.
