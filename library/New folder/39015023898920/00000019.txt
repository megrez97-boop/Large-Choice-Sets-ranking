Chapter II. The Classification of Designs
A. Group Divisible Designs
1. Introduction. The largest, simplest and perhaps most important class of D(2) designs is known as
group divisible. A group divisible (GD) design is a D(2) design for which the treatments may be divided
into m groups of n distinct treatments each such that treatments that belong to the same group are first
associates and two treatments that belong to different groups are second associates. The group divisible
association scheme is an mXn array of the treatment symbols (1, 2, mn=v, say) such that rows of
the array are groups.
