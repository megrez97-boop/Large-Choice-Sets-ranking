3. Classification of D(2) designs. This volume contains more than 800 experimental designs of type
D(2) for which 2 ≤ k ≤ 10 and 2 <r< 10 where k is the number of experimental units in a block and r is
the number of blocks in which each treatment appears. These designs are classified into the following
types:
(a) Group Divisible (GD), consisting of three subtypes
(al) Singular (S)
(a2) Semi-Regular (SR)
(a3) Regular (R)
(b) Triangular (T)
(c) Latin Square types (LS)
(d) Cyclic (C)
(e) Partial Geometry (PG)
(f) Miscellaneous (M).
These classifications are defined and briefly described in the next chapter. General least squares
analysis of D(2) designs is provided in the third chapter along with a general method of solution suitable for
modern computers. Methods for use with a desk calculator are also illustrated.
4. Scope of the tables. The extensive expansion of the present classification of D(2) designs from 375
to more than 800 parametric specifications necessitated the introduction of new modes of presentation to
conserve space and to minimize publication costs. Simultaneously, it has been attempted to provide con-
siderable auxiliary information not heretofore provided in such compilations of designs. To efficiently use
this volume the reader should be aware of the tabular subdivisions, their purpose and scope. There are 20
tables designated by 0 and the roman numerals I, II, . . ., XIX.
Table 0. Auxiliary Table for Identifying Designs of Potential Interest According to the Magnitudes
of Parameters k and v.
Table 0 is the key guide to entry of Tables IV through XIX. It is felt that the user of this volume, when he
is searching for a design, will generally know the block size k and the number v of treatments for his pro-
posed experiment. Thus Table 0 has 9 subheadings "k=2", "k=3”, . . ., “k= 10". Under each subheading
the entries are for increasing magnitudes of v, the number of treatments. Under k, v combinations, the entries
are for the number of replications r and the number b of blocks. The table entries are design reference
numbers having the combination of values k, v, r, b. Knowing these design reference numbers and the titles
for Tables IV through XIX, the user is guided to designs of interest. For instance, if the user is searching
for an experimental arrangement with blocks of size k=4 to accommodate v = 10 treatments and is willing
to have an r as large as 10, entry of Table 0 first for k=4, then v = 10 leads to the following designs for r=2,
