E. Partially Balanced Designs Based on Partial Geometries
1. Partial geometries. A partial geometry (r, k, t) has been defined by Bose (1963) as a system of unde-
fined points and lines and an undefined relation called incidence satisfying the following four axioms:
(i) Any two points are incident with not more than one line.
(ii) Each point is incident with r lines.
(iii) Each line is incident with k points.
(iv) If the point P is not incident with the line l, then there pass through P exactly t lines (t ≥ 1) inter-
secting l.
