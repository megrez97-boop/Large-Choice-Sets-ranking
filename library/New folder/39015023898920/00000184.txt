Complement: Not listed, r = 72
Dual: The BIB design (81,90,10,9,1), Roy and

Groups: (10,9)
Plan:
To construct the b = 81 blocks of this
design one can start with a solution for
the symmetrical balanced incomplete block
design (v, b, r, k, λ) = (91, 91, 10, 10, 1). It
is known [see Marshall Hall, Jr., J. Dean
Swift and Raymond Killgrove (1959)]
that there exist four solutions for this
design, but one solution may be found in
T. Kitagwa and M. Mitome (1955). From
this design select for deletion 10 blocks
containing a common treatment (say the
treatment labeled 91). Upon removal of
the 10 chosen blocks, strike out the
common treatment (91) and use the 10
subsets so obtained as the 'groups' for
the desired GD design. The set of 81
residual blocks constitute a solution for
design SR110. The groups will not be in
the standardized form, and therefore,
relabeling of treatments both in the plan
and in the groups would be required if
standardization is desired.
Since there exist 4 distinct solutions
for the SBIB design and since the deletion
of blocks from each of them can be done in
91 ways, it should be simple, though
tedious, to construct several independent
plans for this GD design.
