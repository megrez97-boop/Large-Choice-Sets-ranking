5. Computer analysis. In this section the analysis of partially balanced block designs is given in a
general form suitable for programming an electronic computer. The three models of section 2 are discussed
separately in subsections, treating separately the case where the blocks are regarded as parameters (intra-
block analysis) and the case where the block values are regarded as random variables (inter-block analysis).
Partitioned matrices are used to show ways to reduce the size of matrices to be inverted. In the case of one
or more missing observations it may be possible to solve by use of linear regression techniques on the
observation equations.
In this section and in section 6, matrices and vectors are denoted by bold face letters; elements of
matrices are denoted by the corresponding lower case letters, e.g., C= (cij). Otherwise, notation is con-
sistent with the foregoing sections unless specially defined in these sections.
