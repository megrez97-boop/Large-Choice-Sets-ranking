2.1. Intra-block analyses. In this section we shall present the formulas necessary for the intra-block
analyses and the expressions for the computational constants C1, C2, H, and ▲ and the efficiency factors
E₁, E2, and E. In section 2.2 we specify the formulas for the combined intra- and inter-block analyses.
Let there be v treatments whose effects it is required to compare by using a partially balanced design
with two associate classes. We divide the N(=bk) experimental units to which the treatments are to be
applied into b more or less homogeneous sets called blocks, each block containing k units. The design di-
vides the treatments into b sets, such that each set contains k treatments, and each treatment occurs in r
sets, other conditions for a D(2) design being satisfied. The b sets of treatments are assigned randomly to
the b blocks, and the individual treatments of a set are assigned randomly to the units of the corresponding
block. Let yy be the observed result of applying the ith treatment to a unit in the jth block. We delay consid-
eration of the conceptual composition of the observation y until the three models are discussed separately.
It should be noted that to a given j(j=1, 2, . . ., b) only k different values of i are associated, since the
symbol y or ty is meaningful only when the ith treatment occurs in the jth block.
