2. Partial geometries which are D(2) designs. It is known (Bose (1963)) that a partial geometry (r, k,
r-1), r≥2 [which is known in certain mathematical areas as a net (r, k) of degree r and order k] is the
same as the lattice design of r replications of Yates (1936) or a D(2) design with blocks of size k=r and r
replications based on the Latin square type L,(r) association scheme. Due to the duality theorem and the
fact that certain SRGD designs and finite Euclidian planes can be constructed from certain L,(n) associ-
ation schemes [Clatworthy (1967)], it follows that many designs we have classified as SRGD designs and
LS designs are also partial geometries.
The partial geometries (2, k, 2), where k=n-1, are known to be triangular designs in which the
blocks are the n rows (columns) of the triangular association scheme with parameter n, i.e., the family
(2.20) of triangular designs of section B of this chapter. The duals of these designs are the well known
unreduced BIB designs of the family
v=n, b= n(n-1)/2,
r=n-1, k = 2, λ = 1.
Thus the unreduced BIB designs with k=2 and certain triangular designs could be classified as partial
geometries.
Certain other designs first constructed by Clatworthy (1954), Bose and Clatworthy (1955), Seiden (1961),
Ray-Chaudhuri (1962), Shrikhande (1965) and Benson (1966) are also known to be partial geometries. [See
the Literature Sources of Table XVI for identification of particular cases with r and k less than 10.]
3. The simple type of D(2) design and the present classification of designs. The design category of BCS
(1954) having one λ equal to zero and labeled "simple" was a convenience classification into which designs
not otherwise classified could be put in 1954. The first three of these designs [Sl. 1, 2 and 3] have long been
known to be invalid and were discarded. Designs Sl. 18, Sl. 19, Sl. 20 and Sl. 26 also had invalid solutions
which were detected by the National Bureau of Standards computer program devised to test the validity
of designs considered for this volume. Designs Sl. 18, Sl. 19, Sl. 20 and Sl. 26 were reconstructed to provide
valid solutions. Except for the discarded Design Sl. 1, all the simple designs of BCS (1954) having λ, = 1
and λ=0 can be classified as partial geometries. Hence these designs and some new partial geometries
[namely, designs PG 1, PG 5, PG 8, PG 16, and PG 17] were put together to form the new classification
of our Tables XVI and XVII for partially balanced designs based on partial geometries. While there are
many partial geometries which are not included in Tables XVI and XVII, many that have r and k less than
or equal to ten are included in other classifications.
