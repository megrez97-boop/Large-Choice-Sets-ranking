In BCS (1954) one plan or solution is presented for each of the 124 possible parametric specifications
for the SGD designs of the subclass defined by r≤ 10. Due to the combinatorial uniqueness of the balanced
incomplete block [briefly D(1)] designs on which the SGD designs necessarily depend, there are 93 cases
for which the BCS (1954) plans are unique (up to an isomorphism) and hence completely solved. In the
present publication, Tables IV and V contain multiple plans or solutions for 20 of the 28 cases for which it
is known that multiple (combinatorially distinct) plans are possible. There are two cases for which uniqueness
is undecided in that the uniqueness status of the corresponding D(1) design is still unknown.
Table IV does not contain a "Literature Sources" section because the source is either BCS (1954) or
the additional (solutions) plans are given for the first time in this volume.
4. Construction of semi-regular group divisible designs. Bose, Shrikhande and Bhattacharyya (1953)
[hereafter briefly referred to as BSB (1953)] have made a far-reaching but not exhaustive study of the con-
struction of group divisible designs. Their treatment of the SRGD designs is highly systematic and utilizes
in many instances the combinatorial arrangements known as orthogonal arrays. Their work forms the basic
source for all SRGD designs given by BCS (1954), which included all parametric specifications, except for
one inadvertent omission, of the subclass of SRGD designs having 3 ≤ k ≤ 10 and 2 < r < 10. For these 91
cases BCS (1954) gave one solution or plan each. However, these designs are also capable of multiple solu-
tions in 22 instances and these are indicated in our Table VI and presented in our Table VII. It was deter-
mined that there are 28 parametric specifications for SRGD designs which give unique solutions including
the 17 cases for which the blocks are of size k=2. The latter designs were constructed by Clatworthy (1955)
who also provided new solutions for designs SR 24, 29, 51, 110. Other distinct plans were taken from publica-
tions by Bose and Nair (1939), Connor (1952), BSB (1953) [to replace the invalid solution for BCS (1954)
design SR 31], P. M. Roy (1954b, 1954c), John (1966), Clatworthy (1967) and adapted for the standardization
of association schemes and mode of display utilized in the present publication. There are 56 cases for which
the uniqueness status is unknown.
