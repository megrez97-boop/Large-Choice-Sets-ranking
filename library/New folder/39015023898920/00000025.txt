5. Construction of regular group divisible designs. BCS (1954) contains 68 constructions for the same
number of parametric specifications; these appear to depend heavily on BSB (1953) and upon the original
paper by Bose and Nair (1939). The BCS (1954) design designated R4 was found to be invalid but correctable
and their designs R56 and R60 contained typographical errors which were also corrected. The 68 designs
were also included in the present Tables VIII and IX. Besides these, other RGD designs were taken from the
literature and adapted to the present volume. Designs were taken from Bose and Nair (1939), Bannerjee
(1951), BSB (1953), P. M. Roy (1954c), Archbold (1955), Clatworthy (1955, 1956, 1967), Sprott (1955, 1959),
Archbold and Johnson (1956), Thompson (1956), Freeman (1957), Liu (1963), Liu and Chang (1964), David and
Wolock (1965), John (1966), Ikeda (1967, 1967b) and Masuyama (1967). Designs in this category appear to
be highly irregular in their block and treatment structures. Consequently, it does not seem likely that a few
general construction procedures are capable of yielding the plans provided here for 209 parametric speci-
fications having r, k≤ 10. The construction techniques employed in the literature display a wide spectrum
of ingenuity. This diversity may be explainable by the fact that the RGD designs are the only GD designs
for which the determinant |NN' | is positive.
Symmetrical regular group divisible designs very frequently have duals which are themselves RGD
designs with the same parameters, i.e., they are self-dual. Many such designs, both RGD and certain other
types of D(2) designs, have been identified by use of a theorem due to Hoffman (1963). However, in many
cases the author has been able to extend beyond Hoffman's "self-dual" conclusion to the stronger "truly
self-dual" conclusion. Entries to the "Dual Designs" columns of the "even" numbered Tables VIII, XII,
XIV, and XVIII and entries opposite "Dual” in the “odd” numbered Tables IX, XIII, XV, and XIX indicate
particular designs for which the above remarks apply.
