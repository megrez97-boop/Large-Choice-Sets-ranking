Most of the miscellaneous designs are characterized by one zero-valued A but there are 8 instances of
designs that have both A's different from zero. In the latter case the association schemes are of no assistance
in analyzing data arising from these designs. The reader is referred to Rao (1947) who gives general methods
for analysis of incomplete block designs. For those designs having one zero-valued λ the blocks can serve
as association schemes, and then the analysis procedures found in BCS (1954) for the simple type of D(2)
designs are appropriate.
Little is known about uniqueness among the plans for miscellaneous designs. In 21 instances the para-
metric specifications for designs are known to be capable of multiple distinct solutions. Thus the uniqueness
status is an open question for 25 of the 46 parametric specifications.
