2.1.2. Model 2: Subsets of blocks as replications. In many designs, it is possible to group the blocks
in such a way, that within every group each treatment is replicated n times. These groups may be called
replication groups. If there are r' such groups, then nr' = r. The experimental material in this case should
be so divided that the blocks to which the treatment sets of the same replication group are assigned, are
relatively more alike. Thus in agronomic experiments, the land may be divided into r' large pieces corres-
ponding to the replication groups, and then each piece is subdivided into b' =b/r' blocks. In actually laying
down the experiment, one makes a three-fold randomization in the assignment of (i) the replication groups to
groups of blocks, (ii) the sets of treatments in a replication group to the individual blocks of the block groups,
and (iii) the treatments of a set to the units in the corresponding block. Suppose that the ith treatment is
