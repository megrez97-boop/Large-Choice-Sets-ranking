listed references in brackets-Whenever listed references are contained in brackets, [], in Tables VI, VIII, X, . it is intended
to convey the assertion that the particular plan, solution or construction of the author referred to is isomorphic with the con-
struction provided by the author listed immediately to the left of the brackets. For example, the reference "Bose and Nair
(1939) [Clatworthy (1967), John (1966)], Freeman (1957)" would mean that the solutions published by Clatworthy (1967) and
John (1966) can be put into isomorphism with the solution published by Bose and Nair (1939) but that the solution of Freeman
(1957) is possibly distinct.
